Camping in West Virginia

There's no much better location to get in touch with the wild and camp than one of the best campgrounds in West Virginia. Outdoor camping is among the most popular activities at West Virginia's forests and state parks. There are more than 1,500 camping areas at 27 various forests and state parks. There is a camping experience available for everybody.

Wouldn't it be great to be surrounded by forest views with stars overhead at resorts like the Coopers Rock State Forest or the Audra State Park or Coopers Rock State Forest. Trying to find a distinctively West Virginian experience? Attempt yurt outdoor camping at the Tomlinson Run State Park or remain over night in the "brought back to life" Thorny Mountain Fire Tower at Seneca State Forest.

  1. Audra Camping

    2. Audra State Park is a greatly woody location by the Middle Fork River situated in southwestern Barbour County and a part of Upshur County. There are a total of 65 campsites that has two great bathhouses and a playground for kids to enjoy.

    The Audra's campground is open to everyone from spring through fall. This is if weather condition allows it. This is a popular location so campgrounds might be all booked, so be sure to book your reservation ahead of time.

    Amenities available
    • Camping
    • Hiking
    • Boating
    • Swimming
    • Geocaching
    • Biking

  2. Spruce Knob Lake Campground, Dry Fork

    3. Spruce Knob Lake Camping site lies west of Spruce Knob, which is the highest point in West Virginia. Close by is the Seneca Creek Backcountry. The Seneca Creek Backcountry has 60 miles of routes all ready to be checked out. For people trying to find outdoor camping nearby, there is the Gatewood Campground.

    If you like river or stream fishing then you can head over to the popular location Gandy Creek. Fishing is readily available at Spruce Knob Lake for everyone. The area was developed in 1952. The plan was to make it a better location for fishing. You will find a parking lot, small boat launch, and vault toilets here.

  3. Greenbrier State Forest Camping

    4. This State Forest covers over 5,200 acres in southeastern West Virginia. Greenbrier State Forest provides remote natural charm with a large range of leisure choices like fishing, , hiking, swimming, and mountain cycling. The forest is very close to the historical and captivating towns of Lewisburg and White Sulphur Springs, so visitors can take pleasure in a day of privacy communicating with nature, then go out for dining, shopping and the arts. With camping and cabins, Greenbrier State Forest is likewise a perfect location for bicyclists on the surrounding Greenbrier River Path.

    P.S: When taking a trip to Greenbrier State Forest, campers and vehicles will be required to take a trip through Harts Run Tunnel, which is 13' 6" high.

  4. Babcock Camping

    5. 20 miles north of West Virginia's renowned New River Gorge Bridge, the Babcock State Park gives its visitors 4,127 acres of tranquil, yet rugged charm. A quick streaming trout stream winds throughout a boulder-strewn and beautiful canyon. The Babcock State Park is mostly known for the creek known as the Glade Creek Grist Mill. This is a completely practical reproduction of the initial Cooper's Mill. Other destinations consist of leisure activities like fishing, hiking and boating.

    Amenities available
    • 52-unit camping site, 28 campsitessites with electrical connections, 4 of which you can access and 24 non-electric campsites.
    • Accommodates trailers or camping tents and has a main bathhouse with a laundry that is coin-operated.
    • 2 sewage centers and 2 water fill-up stations.
    • Ice and Fire wood are readily available for you to purchase.
    • WiFi is offered.
    • Mobile phone service is offered from the majority of mobile phone companies.

  5. Blackwater Falls State Park

    6. The Blackwater Falls State Park's amber-colored waters plunge 5 stories then topple and twist through an 8-mile long canyon. The "black" water is an outcome of tannic acid that has fallen fallen red spruce needles and hemlock. This location is among the most photographed campsites or campgrounds in West Virginia. Campers checking out the park can take pleasure in activities like fishing, hiking, cycling, and many more.

    The camping area is open from the end of April through to October 31, weather condition allowing. Starting each November, non-electric (sites 31-65) and the shower houses are closed for the winter season. Campsites 1-30 are open for all types of primitive camping.

Conclusion

There is no doubt that you will enjoy staying for a couple days in West Virginia. It is a great place to enjoy time away for home.
